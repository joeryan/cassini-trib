Cassini Team

A tribute to the members of the Cassini Space probe mission

During the almost 20 year mission, 260 scientists from 17 countries worked together to better understand Saturn and it's moons. Additionally, thousands of others worked to design, build, launch the probes. Thier journey began even before the 1997 launch, as a working group to explore cooperative miisions in 1982. It culminated with the final flight of the Cassini probe into the atmosphere of Saturn.

Scientists will continue to review and explore the huge amount of data gathered by the Cassini and Huygens probe. The probe orbited Saturn 293 times before it's final pass and entry into the atmosphere.

  • 1982 - Initial discussions about cooperative missions
  • 15 October 1997 - Launched aboard a Titan IVB/Centaur
  • 26 April 1998 & 24 Jun 1999 - Flyby of Venus
  • 18 August 1999 - Flyby of Earth
  • 30 December 2000 - Flyby of Jupiter
  • 1 July 2004 - entered orbit around Saturn
  • 15 September 2017 -Entry into Saturn's atmosphere and mission termination

Learn more about the Cassini team on the Nasa Cassini Team page and the Cassini-Huygens mission Wikipedia page.
